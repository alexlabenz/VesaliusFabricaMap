Mapping the Fabrica

Alex Labenz - aplabenz@arizona.edu

HIST 696F - Early Modern Europe

This is the first iteration of a digital history mapping project tracking the spread of Andreas Vesalius' De Humani Corporis Fabrica Libri Septem and its imitations across Europe. This visualization uses data collected from the University of St. Andrews' Universal Short Title Catalog and a wide range of online library catalogs, namely the Italian National Census of Sixteenth-Century Italian Editions (EDIT16) and Online Public Access Catalogue del Servizio Bibliotecario Nazionale (OPAC-SBN), the French Syst√®me Universitaire de Documentation (SUDOC), the National Library of the Netherlands' KB-catalogus, the United Kingdom's Jisc Library Hub Discover, the Verzeichnis der im deutschen Sprachbereich erschienenen Drucke des 16, 17 and 18. Jahrunderts (VD 16, VD 17, and VD 18) administrated by the Bavarian State Library, and the Karlsruhe Institute of Technology's Karlsruher Virtueller Katalog. The color of each point reflects the publication date of the earliest copy of the Fabrica from that city within the dataset. Please mouse over points to view more specific details!
